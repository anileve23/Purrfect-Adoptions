Frequently Asked Questions:


  • What is the adoption process like?

    Answer: Our adoption process involves filling out an application, meeting with our adoption counselors, and completing a home visit to ensure a suitable environment for the cat.

  • What are the adoption fees?

    Answer: Adoption fees vary depending on the age, breed, and medical needs of the cat. However, our fees typically cover spaying/neutering, vaccinations, and microchipping.

  • Are the cats available for adoption vaccinated and spayed/neutered?

    Answer: Yes, all cats available for adoption are vaccinated, spayed/neutered, and receive a thorough medical examination before being placed in their forever homes.

  • Do you offer post-adoption support?

    Answer: Absolutely! We provide resources, guidance, and advice to assist with the adjustment period after adoption. Our team is always available to answer any questions or address concerns that may arise.

  • Can I adopt if I live in an apartment?

    Answer: Yes, living in an apartment doesn't necessarily disqualify you from adopting. We assess each adoption application individually to ensure that the living situation is suitable for the cat's needs.

  • What if my adopted cat has behavioral issues?

    Answer: We offer support and guidance to address any behavioral issues that may arise after adoption. Our goal is to ensure a successful and harmonious relationship between you and your new feline companion.

  • Can I adopt if I have other pets at home?

    Answer: Yes, we consider households with existing pets for adoption. We conduct meet-and-greet sessions to assess compatibility and ensure a smooth transition for all animals involved.

  • What happens if I can no longer care for my adopted cat?

    Answer: If circumstances change and you can no longer care for your cat, we request that you return the cat to us. We are committed to the lifelong well-being of all cats adopted through our organization.
